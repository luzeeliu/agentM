This specialisation is designed to give students the opportunity to develop their area of expertise in Criminal Justice and Criminology by completing four courses (24 UOC) from the prescribed elective courses in Criminal Justice and Criminology specialisation course list.
When a student completes the Master of Laws which incorporates a specialisation, the student's academic transcript and Testamur will identify the specialisation and will contain the words 'Master of Laws in [the specialisation completed]' or words to that effect.

about Overview
Learning Outcomes
Expand all
1.
Analyse the legal and criminological frameworks, principles and theories underpinning the criminal justice system at both the national and international levels.
keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Examine and critique the role of the criminal justice system and alternative justice processes in responding to and preventing harm in the community.
keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Conduct advanced legal and criminological research that effectively uses primary and secondary sources to apply ethical judgement about the operation of the criminal justice system.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Communicate knowledge of Criminal Justice and Criminology in both written and oral forms to a range of audiences.
