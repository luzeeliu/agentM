This studio course introduces you to the key creative and professional principles for making art in the public realm. You will learn how to effectively respond to public art briefs, develop site and context specific approaches, and produce self-initiated artworks for formal public exhibition. With professional practice skills and experiences embedded, this course prepares you to engage with public art frameworks and opportunities in contemporary art contexts. Project planning, fieldwork, conceptual development, studio experimentation, and documentation are all important skills that you will develop as you engage in hands-on experiences developing your own public art practice.
