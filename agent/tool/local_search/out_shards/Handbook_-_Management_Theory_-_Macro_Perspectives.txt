This class centres on critical analysis, discussion and reflection on key studies regarding theory and research in Organization Theory, Innovation, Entrepreneurship, Strategy. As an academic field of study, organization theory and strategic management seeks to develop comprehensive understanding about the antecedents and consequences of a wide range of organizational activities relating to firm behavior, performance, and competitive advantage. Students will review and critically discuss major theories, developments and debates in these fields, and how theory relates to empirical research.
Specifically, the course aims to introduce the key issues in theory development, develop understanding of the role of theory in informing and guiding empirical analysis, expose students to critical debates in theory and theory development, and develop and apply fundamental theoretical and related research skills
