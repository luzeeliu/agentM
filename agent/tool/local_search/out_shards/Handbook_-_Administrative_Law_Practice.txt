Administrative Law Practice
focuses on government decision making.
The course covers the relevant skills, values and competencies for the optional practice area of Administrative Law Practice as prescribed by the Legal Profession Admission Rules 2015, Schedule 2 (Practical legal training competencies for entry-level lawyers).
Course content includes obtaining information under freedom of information legislation, obtaining the review of administrative decisions, and representing a client before courts and administrative tribunals.
This course is an elective course for the Graduate Diploma in Legal Professional Practice (GDLPP). Students must be enrolled in the GDLPP to enrol in this course and must have completed PLTX1020 Skills in Practice.
