The School of Health Sciences Honours full-time program is run over three terms. It provides advanced research training in health science research, including the disciplines of Exercise Science, Exercise Physiology, Physiotherapy, Dietetics, Nutrition, and Pharmacy. You will undertake a supervised research project that places emphasis on advanced disciplinary knowledge, the use of specialised techniques relevant to your chosen research area, critical thinking and scientific communication. You will develop skills in oral presentation by presenting your work in research seminars, and skills in scientific writing via completion a research proposal and a research manuscript based on substantial independent research activity. The Program is also comprised of compulsory Research Skills educational content, School of Health Sciences (SoHS) research seminars and Departmental/Research Institute/Lab Group seminars throughout the Program.
