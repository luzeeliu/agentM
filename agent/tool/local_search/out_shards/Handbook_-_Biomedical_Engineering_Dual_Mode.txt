Overview
The specialisation is designed for students who are completing the Master of Biomedical Engineering program as part of a double degree program with a Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) in the following specialisations only:

Learning Outcomes
Expand all
1.
Apply comprehensive and specialised knowledge in biomedical engineering, integrating principles from both engineering and biological sciences.
keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Interpret and explain the impact of contextual factors, such as regulatory and ethical considerations, on biomedical engineering practice.
keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Utilise engineering techniques, tools, and resources to develop solutions for complex medical problems.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Collaboratively evaluate biomedical engineering designs and projects, identifying potential improvements and optimisations.
keyboard_arrow_down
5.
Critique the effectiveness of biomedical engineering solutions in real-world applications.
keyboard_arrow_down
6.
Appraise professional and ethical responsibilities in biomedical engineering, adhering to industry standards and practices.
keyboard_arrow_down
7.
Effectively communicate complex biomedical engineering concepts and solutions to diverse stakeholders, including medical professionals, patients, and regulatory bodies.
