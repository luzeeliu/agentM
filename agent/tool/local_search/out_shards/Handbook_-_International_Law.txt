This specialisation is designed to give students the opportunity to develop their area of expertise in International Law by completing four courses (24 UOC) from the prescribed elective courses in International Law specialisation course list.
When a student completes the Master of Laws which incorporates a specialisation, the student's academic transcript and Testamur will identify the specialisation and will contain the words 'Master of Laws in [the specialisation completed]' or words to that effect.

about Overview
Learning Outcomes
Expand all
1.
Analyse the legal frameworks, principles, and theories underpinning international law, including the roles of states, international organisations, and other actors in the global legal system.
keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Examine and critique the evolving role of international law in addressing contemporary global issues and the application of international law to domestic legal systems.
keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Conduct advanced legal research that effectively uses primary and secondary sources, applying professional judgement to assess complex international legal issues and their implications across diverse jurisdictions.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Communicate knowledge of international law in both written and oral forms to a range of audiences, professionally and with precision.
