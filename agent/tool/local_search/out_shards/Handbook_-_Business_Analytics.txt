Business Analytics equips students with the technical and non-technical skills required to produce and communicate actionable findings and insights from an organisation’s data. Students will be introduced to descriptive, predictive and prescriptive analytics for business problems, evaluating policies, and understanding the interplay between financial and non-financial data. In addition to statistical modelling, programming and database management skills, students will be introduced to the ethical and legal issues of data governance. Business Analytics equips graduated with a holistic framework to span the bridge between business-problem owners and technical experts, and to communicate the output of analysis to improve business decision making.

Specialisation Structure
Students must complete 30 UOC.
Students taking a combination of majors and/or minors that require COMP2511 as a core course should not take INFS2822.
Students taking a combination of major and/or minors in Business Analytics and Information Systems can complete either INFS1603 or COMM2822 as the core course and fulfill the major UOC requirement by completing an additional prescribed elective for the major or minor.
