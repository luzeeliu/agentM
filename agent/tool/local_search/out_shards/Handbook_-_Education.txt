The Graduate Certificate in Education degree is designed for qualified teachers and other educational professionals who wish to study education at an advanced level. Commonwealth Supported Places (previously HECS) are available for local candidates.
The program is suitable for teachers, trainers, coaches, adult educators, vocational educators, educational designers, and other professionals in educational settings.
The program offers specialisation in Educational Studies, Curriculum and Assessment, Educational Psychology, Gifted Education, Special Education and TESOL.
Note
:
This program does not offer professional accreditation to teach in primary or secondary schools in NSW or Australia. For pre-service teacher education programs, please see Bachelor of Education (Secondary), Master of Teaching (Primary), and Master of Teaching (Secondary).

Minimum Entry Requirements
keyboard_arrow_down
One of the following:
A bachelor's degree and postgraduate teaching qualification (e.g., Graduate Diploma of Education or Master of Teaching)
A bachelor's degree or combined degree in Education, including professional experience or practicum, equivalent to the qualifications required for Graduate Teacher status in NSW or other Australian jurisdictions
A bachelor's degree or equivalent qualification in a related field, plus one year of relevant professional experience and development
Teaching/Education Qualification:
This is considered a degree or postgraduate teaching qualification in Early Childhood, Primary, Secondary, Adult or higher education and includes practicum/placement.
Accreditation: I
f you hold current graduate teacher accreditation with a recognised Australian professional body (e.g. NESA, BOSTES, VIC Institute of Teachers) you must provide evidence of your accreditation record. Please include accreditation details when uploading your CV.
Related fields:
Arts, humanities, media, psychology, social work, business, economics, languages, linguistics, communications, policy studies, behavioural sciences, social sciences.
Relevant professional experience:
Teacher, educational developer, languages teacher, lecturer, teacher of English to speakers of other languages, educational leader, educational administration. Relevant professional experience does not include periods of Practicum or Work Placement (Work Integrated Learning) undertaken when training to become a teacher.
The following additional entry requirements apply to those students who wish to study the Educational Research specialisation (i.e., EDSTCT) in the Graduate Certificate in Education:
The achievement of at least a WAM of 75 (or equivalent) in the most recently completed degree; AND
Successful performance in an interview where questions will be asked about the student's interest in, and commitment to, studies in research; AND
The availability of a suitable supervisor

Progression Requirements
Articulation arrangements:
The Graduate Certificate in Education forms part of a sequence of articulated programs comprising the Graduate Certificate and Masters degree of Education. A postgraduate student enrolled in an articulated program may progress from the Graduate Certificate to the Masters level with full credit for courses completed in the Graduate Certificate in Education.
