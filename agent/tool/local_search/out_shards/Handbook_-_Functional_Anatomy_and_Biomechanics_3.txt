In this course students will apply anatomical and biomechanical principles to the analysis of movement. Lectures will expand on content introduced in ANAT1451 Functional Anatomy & Biomechanics 1 and ANAT1452 Functional Anatomy & Biomechanics 2. Laboratory classes will offer students an opportunity to engage with biomechanical data collection and analyses, while tutorials will provide an opportunity to build theoretical competency.
