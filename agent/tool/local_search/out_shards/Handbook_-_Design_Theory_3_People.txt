This course introduces theory and strategies for designing for people and particularly addresses understanding of human diversity. Attention is given to access, usability, comfort and performance of the human/product system. Class activities and assignments support learning of stategies for representing and engaging users in the design process, drawing on perspectives of Ergonomics/Human Factors, User Interface (UI) design and Inclusive Design. Students are equipped to investigate human-use implications of their own current and future design practice.
