Are you pursuing actuarial studies and looking to build a strong foundation in financial management? Do you want to develop the skills to interpret financial statements, evaluate corporate investments, and understand how businesses raise and manage capital? Then
COMM1240 – Financial Management for Actuarial Studies
is the perfect course for you!
COMM1240 provides actuarial students with a comprehensive understanding of corporate finance, financial reporting, and business taxation. You will learn how companies finance their operations, manage financial risk, and make investment decisions. The course also explores capital markets, corporate valuation, and governance, ensuring you develop the essential financial literacy required for actuarial practice. Additionally, you will develop an awareness of how issues of sustainability are impacting business management and financial reporting, equipping you with a well-rounded understanding of what it takes to run a successful and responsible business.
This course aligns with the
CB1 – Business Finance
accreditation of the Australian Actuaries Institute, offering a tailored approach for actuarial students.
