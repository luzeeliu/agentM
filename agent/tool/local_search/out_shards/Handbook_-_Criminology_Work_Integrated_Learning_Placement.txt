This course provides you with the unique opportunity to gain practical experience and deeper understanding of the criminal justice and related agencies. Aligned with UNSW’s emphasis on work-integrated learning, this course is a structured and supported work placement designed to give you practical experience in tackling criminal justice challenges through working with an criminal justice/related government or non-government organisation. This will be a one-of a kind experience to help you understand how criminal justice works in practice.
This course is only available to students enrolled in the Bachelor of Criminology and Criminal Justice Program (BCCJ) and students enrolled in the Criminology Major in the Bachelor of Arts (BA). Enrolment is by course authority and is subject to a selection process involving a written submission and interview.

Conditions for Enrolment
Prerequisite: 78UOC including CRIM1010, CRIM1011 and CRIM2042, WAM 70, and enrolled in a Criminology major or Criminology & Criminal Justice single or double degree. Students in program 4034 are not eligible to enrol. Enrolment consent required.
