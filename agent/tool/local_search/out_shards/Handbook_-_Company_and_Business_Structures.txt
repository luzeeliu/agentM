This course provides an introduction to the main structures and  principles of corporate and non-corporate business structures in Australia.  Wider policy issues, including the powers and role of regulators, are also examined.  The course will allow students to develop perspectives of how business structures vary from jurisdiction to jurisdiction, and provide them with skills and context to engage with advanced issues in corporate law and governance throughout their degree.
Please note that this course is designed to  provide a comprehensive grounding to LLM students who have not undertaken legal study in a Common Law jurisdiction. It is not intended to satisfy the requirements for admission to practice in Australia and cannot be used for credit in the LLB or Juris Doctor programs.
This course is not intended for students holding a Bachelor of Law or Juris Doctor qualification from an Australian or common law institution.
