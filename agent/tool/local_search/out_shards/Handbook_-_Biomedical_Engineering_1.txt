Biomedical Engineering is the application of engineering principles to developing technologies and solving problems in a diverse range of health care related fields e.g. implantable bionics, drug delivery systems, medical imaging, radiotherapies, orthopedic devices, telemedicine, robotic surgery, cell and tissue engineering, records management, physical rehabilitation and others.
The Master of Biomedical Engineering is only available to students enrolled in the 3768 BE(Hons)/MBiomedE or 3133 BE(MatSci)(Hons)/MBiomedE programs. It cannot be taken as a standalone degree.

Learning Outcomes

3.
Utilise engineering techniques, tools, and resources to develop solutions for complex medical problems.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Critically analyse and evaluate biomedical engineering designs and projects, identifying potential improvements and optimisations.
keyboard_arrow_down
5.
Assess and critique the effectiveness and ethical implications of biomedical engineering solutions in real-world applications.
keyboard_arrow_down
6.
Demonstrate a deep understanding of professional and ethical responsibilities in biomedical engineering, exhibiting respect for industry standards and practices.
keyboard_arrow_down
7.
Effectively communicate complex biomedical engineering concepts and solutions to diverse stakeholders, including medical professionals, patients, and regulatory bodies.
