The
Bachelor of Education (Secondary)
[BEd (Sec)] is a program for intending secondary school teachers. It is designed to be combined with another appropriate Bachelor's degree (e.g. Bachelor of Arts, Science, Commerce, Economics, Fine Arts, or Design) to provide students with a nationally accredited pre-service qualification in one or two approved secondary teaching specialisations. The Bachelor of Education covers both theoretical and practical aspects of education, including 80 days supervised professional experience in at least two different secondary schools. The UNSW BEd (Sec) can only be completed full-time or part-time as a double degree (the equivalent of two years of full-time study). When taken as a double degree, the Bachelor of Education has a depth component but no breadth component.
The Depth component is:
The completion of Education Core courses (66 UOC - 10 courses)
The completion of prescribed Education method courses (24 UOC - 4 courses)
The completion of one prescribed Education elective (6 UOC - 1 course)

Major and Minor specialisation requirements in double degree programs
Students must carefully select courses in their non-Education degree to meet the requirements for teaching in specific areas of specialisation.
Students should check the teaching specialisation requirements for the non-Education degree with the School of Education to ensure they provide appropriate background for two single or one double secondary teaching specialisations.
New South Wales Education Standards Authority (NESA)
This initial teacher education program is accredited by NESA. NESA requires initial teacher education programs to be continually updated to meet industry demands. Updates to requirements will apply to all students in this program regardless of commencement date. The School of Education will advise all existing students about how they will meet NESA graduate teacher requirements throughout the program.
