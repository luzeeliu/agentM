Digital technology has transformed our experience of social and political life with the rise of social media, virtual communities and artificial intelligence along with the datification of the human experience. This minor in Digital Social Sciences offers you an opportunity to consider how the operation of power in the digital age affects the relationship between self and society, nation states and global markets, and public and private life.  In this minor, you will learn how digital tools create new opportunities for surveillance, activism and self-tracking. You will learn practical research skills to investigate new digital worlds, appreciate how power organises digital data, and understand how digital media has transformed your relation to self and others.  You will examine the use of digital tools by social movements, and consider broader social justice concerns with respect to solidarity, surveillance, and sovereignty.
