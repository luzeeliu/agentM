In this program, you will be introduced to the necessary theoretical knowledge and practical skills to work as competent and ethical interpreters and translators in international or domestic settings. The areas of specialisation covered in the program are legal, medical, business, media, and community. The academic content of the program is complemented by professional practical experience where you will able to directly apply what was learned in class in real-life dynamic professional settings. In addition to the knowledge and skills of interpreting and translation disciplines and professions, you will also learn related academic and practical skills such as critical analysis and reflection, independent preparation and practice, project management and teamwork – all of which are critical to the work of interpreters and translators. The program is staffed by leading researchers and practitioners in interpreting, translation, linguistics and language technology. The program is also endorsed by the National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) and also prepares you to sit certification exams administered by NAATI for qualification as a Certified Provisional Interpreter, Certified Interpreter, and Certified Translator. With certain coursework, our program will also prepare you to sit specialist certification exams for Certified Advanced Translator, Certified Specialist Interpreter (Health), Certified Specialist Interpreter (Legal) and Certified Conference Interpreter. The program is offered in the following language combinations, subject to student demand: English and Chinese (Mandarin), Chinese (Cantonese), French, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, Russian, Serbian, Spanish and Turkish.

Minimum Entry Requirements
keyboard_arrow_down
Bachelor degree (or equivalent qualification) in any discipline and an advanced level of bilingual proficiency*.
You will be considered to have an advanced level of bilingual proficiency if you have:
Completed 3-year+ qualification equivalent to an Australian Bachelor degree which was undertaken in the nominated language, completed within the 10 years prior to commencement of the program applied for OR
Provided evidence of a NAATI certification/accreditation for interpreting or translation for the nominated language offered in this program OR
Successfully completed TAFE Diploma or TAFE Advanced Diploma of Interpreting OR
Successfully completed UNSW's I&T courses (MODL5100, MODL5101, MODL5102, MODL5103, MODL5104, MODL5107, MODL5108, MODL5112) OR
Provided evidence of being a native speaker of the language and completed high school in the nominated language for this program. High school transcript and proof of completion are required.
Students who have completed the short courses below are eligible to receive up to 6 UoC per course towards their program subject to the requirements and limitations specified by the UNSW Recognition of Prior Learning Procedure.

SC0003 Advanced Legal Interpreting -> MODL5101 Interpreting in Legal Settings
SC0004 Introduction to Simultaneous Interpreting in Conference Settings -> MODL5105 Conference Interpreting
SC0005 Introduction to Translation Technology -> MODL5107 Translation Technology
