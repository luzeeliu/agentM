NOTE:
This course was previously identified as MBAX6272.
The focus in
Change Skills
is on you and your skills development as a change agent.
In order to gain maximum benefit from the course, it is important to:
use the Change Skills Audit to evaluate your skills and to obtain feedback from others who have seen something of your work as a change agent
continually apply the ideas in the course in reflecting on your skills, on ways of more skilfully handling the challenges of organisational change, and on ways to develop your skills
translate your reflections into action plans
create or take opportunities to implement your action plans during the course.

Delivery Format
Standard (usually weekly or fortnightly)
Indicative Contact Hours
1.5hr online class per week
Notes
Our online synchronous mode is for students who like regular live learning sessions with others but who cannot travel to our face-to-face venues. Students must have a reliable internet connection and a working laptop/computer with camera, and attend the scheduled 90-minute online classes in Weeks 1 to 10. There are also other required online asynchronous activities outside of class times.
Workload expectation is 150 hours across the term.

Notes
Our online asynchronous mode is for students who want maximum flexibility to determine when during the week they study. Students must engage in weekly online asynchronous discussion forums and other activities. There are some optional webinars or drop-in group video calls (recorded for those who cannot attend).
Workload expectation is 150 hours across the term.

keyboard_arrow_down
Delivery Mode
In-person
Delivery Format
Intensive
Indicative Contact Hours
32 hours over two weekends
Notes
Our face-to-face intensive mode is for students who prefer face-to-face classes but may need to travel further to come to our face-to-face venues. Interactive classes are held on two non-consecutive weekends, 9am to 5pm each day. There are also required online asynchronous activities before and after each weekend.
Workload expectation is 150 hours across the term.
