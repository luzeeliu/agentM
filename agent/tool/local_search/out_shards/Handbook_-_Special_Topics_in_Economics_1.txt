This course introduces advanced tools and topics in behavioural economics with emphasis on markets, game theory, and individual decision making. The course is intended both for students who wish to develop a solid background in behavioural economics to pursue research in applied fields, and for students who seek to specialise in economic theory.
The course covers a broad array of topics analysed through the lens of behavioural economics. Most time will be spent on developing new tools, but economic applications will also be covered.
