The course explores the wide range of forensic mental health systems that exist, and the institutions that receive input from forensic mental health services. Course content will examine the different models of care underpinning these systems, as well as the essential components and roles which are involved in planning a comprehensive mental health service. It aims to provide a detailed understanding of the complex network that is involved in the care of mentally ill people who are in contact with the criminal justice system, including the important role of clinical corporate governance and responsibilities of staff, and human rights in forensic mental health care.
Topics covered during the course include the functioning and roles of: the State Parole Authority (NSW); the Serious Offenders Review Council (NSW); Mental Health Review Tribunal; the Health Care Complaints Commission; Australian and NZ Royal College of Psychiatrists; and the Medical Board. Also covered are: Corrections and the Prison Medical Service; Forensic Mental Health Hospitals and secure facilities; and the forensic aspects of psychiatry in the armed forces.
