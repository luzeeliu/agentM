This double degree program is designed to allow students to gain accreditation as both Engineers and Surveyors through students completing the the Civil Engineering specialisation of the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) 3707 program in combination with the Bachelor of Surveying 3775 program. This allows students flexibility in their future careers.
Students must also undertake a minimum of 60 days of approved Industrial Training as part of the program requirements. Students will be eligible for accreditation as Civil Engineers with Engineers Australia, as well as Surveyors through the Institution of Surveyors NSW and candidates for registration through the NSW Board of Surveying and Spatial Information (BOSSI).

Double Degree Structure
Students must complete 240 UOC (after 72 UOC reduction from double counted courses).
Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) (168 UOC)
-  168 UOC Civil Engineering specialisation (CVENAH)
- At least 60 days of approved Industrial Training experience (ENGG4999)
Bachelor of Surveying (144 UOC)
The Bachelor of Surveying program includes a 144 UOC disciplinary component of:
- 72 UOC Engineering Foundation Core Courses
- 66 UOC Surveying Core Courses
- 6 UOC Professional Electives
Since students complete the Engineering Foundation Core Courses as part of their Civil Engineering (CVENAH) specialisation, these courses will be double counted to reduce the double degree program by 72 UOC to a total of 240 UOC.

Additional Information
Accreditation:
The Engineering component of this double degree has the same accreditation status as the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours). For details please see Program
3707.
The Surveying component of this double degree is recognised by the Board of Surveying and Spatial Information of New South Wales as meeting the requirements for entry as a candidate to become a Registered Surveyor. The degree is also recognised by the Institution of Surveyors, New South Wales, the Geospatial Council of Australia and Engineers Australia, for admission as members.
Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) Progression Requirements:
Students must make satisfactory progress while studying in the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) program. Students must show cause not to be excluded from the program if any of the following occur:
Fail a core course 2 or more times
Fail more than half of the courses attempted, after completing 84 UOC or more of the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) major
Have an overall WAM less than 50.000 with 48 UOC or more of your Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) remaining.
Students must also satisfy UNSW progression requirements. For more information on university policy on progression requirements please visit
