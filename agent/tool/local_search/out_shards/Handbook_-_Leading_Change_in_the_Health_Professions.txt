This course prepares future health professionals to lead change through reflective practice, evidence-based decision-making, advocacy, and quality improvement. You will develop leadership, negotiation, and advocacy skills to address challenges in healthcare delivery. Emphasis is placed on designing and evaluating improvements using recognised quality improvement tools and methodologies such as 'Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA)' and 'Design Thinking'. You will also explore how health professionals' actions can contribute to achieving the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, particularly those related to health equity, access to care, and sustainable health systems. Through interdisciplinary collaboration and real-world case studies, you will learn to advocate for clients, communities, and your profession while promoting ethical, safe, and equitable care.
