The Master of Architecture degree provides an advanced level of architectural education appropriate to contemporary professional practice. For full-time attendance, the program is typically completed in two years. Part-time attendance for 2.7 years is an option for domestic students.
Building on the solid disciplinary foundation established by Bachelor of Architectural Studies, the Master of Architecture provides an opportunity for students to pursue one of four streams: High Performance Architecture, Social Agency, Urban Conditions, and Housing. Pursuing a stream is optional. Students can also elect to complete the program without completing a stream option.

Admission Requirements
Entry Requirements
Expand all
Minimum Entry Requirements
keyboard_arrow_down
Admission is available to students with a degree from an undergraduate program (or equivalent) in Architecture with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65).
Applicants from international universities are required to provide a portfolio of their design work. The portfolio should include sample works from various stages of the applicant's bachelor's degree, text should accompany all drawings/images to explain the projects. The portfolio must be in digital format.
The different categories of eligible applicants and their specific admission requirements are outlined below:
1. 65 credit average (or equivalent) in applicant's undergraduate architecture degree
OR
2. Applicants who have completed the Bachelor of Architectural Studies (or equivalent) but do not meet the entry requirements specified under 1 above may be considered after they have obtained work experience in an architectural office. Work experience will be individually assessed based on the following submission:
A logbook record of a minimum 12 months full-time work or the equivalent (completed after their bachelors degree).
A referee report from the employer
A portfolio of work (including undertaken during this period)
OR
3. UNSW Bachelor of Interior Architecture (Honours) graduates will meet entry requirements for the Master of Architecture 8143 if they complete the architecture elective pathway in the Bachelor in Interior Architecture (3256) degree. Students will need to complete the following courses as part of this pathway, and achieve an overall WAM of 65. These courses will count as electives in their BIA program.
ARCH1311: Architecture Studio 5
ARCH1302: Architecture Studio 6
ARCH1261: Structures and Construction 2
ARCH1322: Architectural History and Theory 3
ARCH1361: Architectural Science and Building Environment 2
Bachelor of Interior Architecture (Honours) graduates who do not meet the above may be considered after they have obtained five years work experience in an architectural office or equivalent. Work experience will be individual assessed based on the following submission:
A logbook of a minimum of 5 years full-time work of the equivalent (completed after their bachelor's degree)
A referee report from the employer
A portfolio of work (including engagement with architectural construction specifically)

Recognition of Achievement
Award with Excellence
open_in_new
Current Students Award with Excellence
For more information, please visit the link above.
The Award with Excellence is awarded in coursework masters programs, including Masters (Extension) but with the exception of Masters (Extended) such as JD and MD, when a Weighted Average Mean (WAM) of at least 80% has been achieved and at least 50% of the requirements of the award are completed at UNSW. All eligible programs will award 'with Excellence' except in special circumstances where approval of Academic Board has been given for a program to opt out.

Additional Expenses and Resources
This is a design-based program, and thus students may be required to purchase equipment or materials to enable them to complete activities and assessment tasks within the program. The Faculty will endeavour to provide such relevant equipment and materials as appropriate, and to keep costs to students to a minimum. Students will need access to a laptop computer with sufficient capacity for the software applications used across the program. The Faculty of Arts, Design and Architecture provides recommended specifications for laptop computers, and computer labs with all required software applications are available. Some courses may involve site visits, which may incur costs for items such as transport, entry fees and guides. Details of these visits and any associated costs will be communicated to you by the respective course convenor at the start of the term.
