This program provides postgraduate students with the opportunity to study two complementary programs offered in UNSW Arts and Social Sciences to create a combined two year Masters program in professional and interdisciplinary areas.
The Combined Masters degree program offers students an interdisciplinary combination of programs that will substantially extend and deepen their existing skills, knowledge and understanding in professionally relevant areas of study and teach advanced skills in analytical thinking and research.
Upon completion, students may be awarded up to two of the following awards:
Master of Education – MEd
Master of Educational Leadership - MEdLead
Master of Translation – MTran
Master of Environmental Management – MEM
Master of Applied Linguistics – MAppLing
Master of Journalism and Communication – MJournComm
Master of Public Relations and Advertising – MPRA
Master of International Relations – MIR
Master of Development Studies – MDS
Master of Public Policy and Governance – MPPG

Notes for Masters of Education
keyboard_arrow_down
If you combine two Masters of Education specialisations, you only need to complete one research methodology and research project in your two Masters specialisations. You can complete any two additional MEd elective courses for your other specialisation.
If you combine a Masters of Education (TESOL) specialisation with a Master of Applied Linguistics you cannot take any LING electives in your MEd.
Notes for Masters in International Relations/Development Studies/ Public Policy and Governance
keyboard_arrow_down
If you combine two Social Sciences specialisations (i.e. International Relations A and/or Development Studies A and/or Public Policy and Governance A), you only need to complete one Research Core Course for your two Masters specialisations. You can complete additional Elective Courses to meet the minimum UOC requirement.
