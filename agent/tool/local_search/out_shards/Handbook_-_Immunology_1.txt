This Immunology course is designed for students majoring in Medical Microbiology, Biotechnology, Biomolecular Science, Medical Science, and other fields related to human health. The course offers students the opportunity to develop their understanding, skills, and critical thinking in the field of immunology.
The course first introduces the components of the immune system, including their development, structures, functions, interactions, and regulations during immune responses. Subsequently, the course introduces the applied and clinical aspects of immunology, including allergy, immunodeficiency, immune system and cancer, vaccination, autoimmunity, engineering antibodies, diagnostic immunology, and immunological research strategies. The practical classes provide students with hands-on experience on critical immunological techniques such as immunological assays. Moreover, the course guides students in critically analyzing immunological matters and literature relevant to the community.
