University of New South Wales’ Master of Physiotherapy and Exercise Physiology is a unique program that will fast track your career to become a Physiotherapist and an Accredited Exercise Physiologist (AEP)* and is the only program of its kind in Australia. This innovative extended masters will prepare you with the necessary knowledge, skills and attributes to successfully pursue a career that combines two complementary professions. The program has a strong emphasis on interprofessional education, evidence-based and reflective practice as well as a substantial professional practice and clinical placement component, right from the first year. You will learn from experienced and accredited physiotherapists and exercise physiologists in a wide range of clinical settings, including musculoskeletal, neurological, cardiorespiratory, intensive care, community and workplace rehabilitation and aged care.
Physiotherapists and exercise physiologists are in high demand in Australia and overseas and find employment in a wide range of clinical and community settings, working with all age groups, including in public and private hospitals, medical centres, private practices, aged care facilities, out-patient rehabilitation centres, mental health settings, schools and educational facilities, research institutes, independent living/respite centres, as well as elite/local level sporting clubs.
This program is only available as part of the integrated Bachelor of Exercise/ Master of Physiotherapy and Exercise Physiology double-degree (3896) and is not offered as a separate standalone program for direct entry.
*Pending accreditation these programs meet the criteria to become registered as a physiotherapist and exercise physiologist set by the Australian Health Practitioner Registration Agency (AHPRA) and Exercise and Sports Science Australia (ESSA).

This program includes placements in public and private hospitals and community practices as well as other Health Facilities in Australia. In order to attend these placements, students will need to complete a criminal record check. International students need both the Australian check as well as a police certificate from their home country or any country in which they have been a citizen or permanent resident since turning 16 years of age.

CPR, First Aid and Hand Hygiene training
NSW Health require all students to complete mandatory online training modules when commencing a clinical placement. Students who fail to satisfy the compliance requirements of NSW Health may be excluded from undertaking a clinical placement. Students are responsible for obtaining the required documents and vaccinations needed to meet compliance requirements and to attend compliance checks for verification.
There are many different stakeholders involved in this degree, including the NSW Government, public and private hospitals and community practices, each with their own policies/procedures and all medical students must comply with NSW Health requirements before commencing clinical/hospital placements. Because health students will be accessing various health facilities, it is very important they are familiar with NSW Health policies.
