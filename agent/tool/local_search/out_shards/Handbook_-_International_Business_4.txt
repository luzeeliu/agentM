The Master of International Business offers students a 2-year comprehensive postgraduate study program in international business. The program is designed for those seeking a professional career in business management in the rapidly changing global context. The program includes a range of core and elective courses, specialisations, and practical experiences designed to prepare you to operate successfully, sustainably, and ethically in a multi-national, multi-cultural environment.
This program is open to students with an undergraduate degree in any field.

Limitations on Recognition of Prior Learning
keyboard_arrow_down
If you have prior studies at Bachelors’ level or higher that are relevant to the program, you may be eligible for credit that would allow you to take fewer subjects. RPL can be granted for up to 50% of the overall program, with a maximum of 24 UOC based on undergraduate studies, and the remaining RPL granted on the basis of postgraduate studies.
Students who have completed postgraduate studies and have not declared a specialisation are eligible for a maximum of 48 UOC. Students who have completed postgraduate studies and declare a specialisation are eligible for 30 UOC only in electives, in addition to the 6 UOC in specialisation course that aligns with their specialisation.
Please note that program core courses are not considered for the purpose of RPL.

Recognition of Achievement
Award with Excellence
open_in_new
Current Students Award with Excellence
For more information, please visit the link above.
The Award with Excellence is awarded in coursework masters programs, including Masters (Extension) but with the exception of Masters (Extended) such as JD and MD, when a Weighted Average Mean (WAM) of at least 80% has been achieved and at least 50% of the requirements of the award are completed at UNSW. All eligible programs will award 'with Excellence' except in special circumstances where approval of Academic Board has been given for a program to opt out.
