Overview
This is an advanced level postgraduate course in probability theory and stochastic processes for mathematics/statistics and data science students. Topics covered include rigorous definition of probability and random variables; convergence of random variables; Poisson and Wiener process, and Ito Calculus.
This is an advanced level postgraduate course in probability theory and stochastic processes for mathematics/statistics and data science students. Topics covered include rigorous definition of probability and random variables; convergence of random variables; Poisson and Wiener process, and Ito Calculus.

Notice: Students in the Graduate Certificate (7659) or Graduate Diploma (5659) who are aiming to transfer into a Master degree should plan their coursework to ensure the courses they take count for their intended Master degree. The MATH5901 course can only be counted toward the 8750 Master of Statistics or 8161 Master of Financial Mathematics programs after receiving prior written permission from the School, due to the exclusion rule with core course MATH5835 that is required in these programs.
