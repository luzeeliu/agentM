This course examines issues of state crime and state violence through a critical criminological lens. It introduces you to the human rights abuses committed by states as well the challenges associated with the establishment of an international system of criminal justice. Through this course you will develop a critical understanding of different forms of state violence as well as how states may, and may not, be held accountable for the harms they produce and the crimes they perpetrate. The course considers topics such as war crimes and genocide, immigration detention, torture and rendition, natural disaster, resistance to violence and state accountability.
