This is an advanced quantitative methods course which uses a project-based approach to consider the analysis, visualisation and communication of large and complex data sets. Through engagement in lecture, laboratories and seminars, students will choose a topic for in-depth instruction in one of the advanced quantitative research endeavours of the School of BEES. The modules cover techniques including meta-analyses, geographic data analyses, population, and systems dynamics, phylogenetics and time series analyses. Whilst there is no assumed knowledge for this course, students will be expected to display analytical and problem-solving skills in accordance with at least 2 years of undergraduate study.
