This specialisation is designed to give students the opportunity to develop their area of expertise in Human Rights Law and Policy by completing four courses (24 UOC) from the prescribed elective courses in Human Rights Law and Policy specialisation course list. This specialisation is designed to give students the opportunity to develop their area of expertise in Human Rights Law and Policy by completing four courses (24 UOC) from the prescribed elective courses in Human Rights Law and Policy specialisation course list.
When a student completes the Master of Laws which incorporates a specialisation, the student's academic transcript and Testamur will identify the specialisation and will contain the words 'Master of Laws in [the specialisation completed]' or words to that effect.

about Overview
Learning Outcomes
Expand all
1.
Analyse the legal frameworks, principles, and theories underpinning human rights law and policy at both the national and international levels.
keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Examine and critique the current and future role of human rights law in addressing contemporary global challenges.
keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Conduct advanced legal research that effectively uses a variety of primary and secondary sources and professional judgement to assess human rights laws and policies and their implications across diverse jurisdictions.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Communicate knowledge of human rights in both written and oral forms to a range of audiences, professionally and with precision.
