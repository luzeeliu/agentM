The double degree, Bachelor of Fine Arts Bachelor of Education (Secondary) (BFA BEd) is a four-year program (192 UOC) for intending secondary school teachers. The program is a nationally accredited pre-service qualification.
The program enables students to pursue a teaching area in Graphics and Multimedia Technology, Music, or Visual Arts. The program covers both theoretical and practical aspects of education, including 80 days supervised professional experience in at least two different secondary schools.
See Academic Rules for details on NESA accreditation requirements.

Specialisation – Animation and Moving Image
keyboard_arrow_down
A specialisation in Animation and Moving Image enables students to pursue a teaching area in Graphics and Multimedia Technology, as well as a first teaching area in Visual Arts.
To meet NESA accreditation requirements for Visual Arts, students in Animation and Moving Image MUST choose one 2D (Visual Effects) AND one 4D disciplinary studio stream (Animation, Sound, Moving Image, Game Art, Game Design or 3D Visualisation) for their Disciplinary Studios.
Specialisation – Music, Music Creative Practice, Music Pedagogy, Music Sonic Arts
keyboard_arrow_down
A specialisation in Music, Music Creative Practice, Music Pedagogy or Music Sonic Arts enables students to pursue a teaching area in Music.
Specialisation – Visual Arts
keyboard_arrow_down
A specialisation in Visual Arts enables students to pursue a teaching area in Visual Arts, with an option to complete a second teaching area in Graphics and Multimedia Technology.
To meet NESA accreditation requirements for Visual Arts, students MUST choose one 2D (Drawing, Painting, Photography or Printmaking) AND one 3D/4D studio (Sculpture (3D), Photography or Moving Image (4D)).
Students planning to take Graphics and Multimedia Technology as a second teaching area MUST take Moving Image or Animation.
