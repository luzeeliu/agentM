This program is only available combined with the 3707 Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) program.
The Bachelor of Surveying is designed to equip you for a career as a professional Surveyor. We provide you with a solid background that will prepare you for learning knowledge and skills in this discipline. You will get the chance to apply yourself to surveying knowledge, application and professional practice. This program will cater for students wishing to gain accreditation as Surveyors as part of an integrated five-year program in double degree mode with the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) 3707. The School of Civil and Environmental Engineering administers the program.
The program is designed to allow students to gain accreditation as both Engineers and Surveyors when taken with the 3707 Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) program as a double degree program.

Professional Outcomes
Professional Recognition
The Bachelor of Surveying is recognised by the Board of Surveying and Spatial Information of New South Wales for registration as a surveyor in NSW. The degree is also recognised by the Institution of Surveyors, New South Wales, the Geospatial Council of Australia and Engineers Australia, for admission as members.
Career Opportunities
Engineer working in the mining industry, construction surveying firms, architecture firms, land management and planning, hydrographic surveying, aerial imaging and cartography.
