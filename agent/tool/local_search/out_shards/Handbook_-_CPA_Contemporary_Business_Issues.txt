This course examines contemporary and emerging global issues in order to familiarise you with the business environment that you will likely be operating in as an accountant. It uses contemporary issues as a vehicle for advancing the development of professional skills, values, ethics and attitudes.
In particular, Contemporary Business Issues is designed to help you develop a future-focused perspective on professional accounting work. It will also highlight the challenges and opportunities for the accounting profession in the contemporary business environment.

Conditions for Enrolment
Prerequisite: (Enrolment in ACCTKS CPA Specialisation AND completion of 36 UOC in Program 8415), OR (Enrolment in 5415). Students must also have completed ACCT5935, ACCT5936, and ACCT5937.
Additional Enrolment Constraints
Students must be an Associate Member of CPA Australia. Students must concurrently enrol in the 'Contemporary Business Issues' module of the CPA Program to gain access to course materials and be eligible to sit the CPA Australia exam for this module.
