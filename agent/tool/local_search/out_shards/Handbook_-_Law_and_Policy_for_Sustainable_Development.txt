This course examines the law and policy system for sustainable development. The course provides students with a breadth of knowledge about contemporary global developments in sustainable development law and policy. It explores sustainable development at multiple levels, across multiple subject matter, and their interaction as a legal system. It examines the Sustainable Development Goals and national level sustainable development law and policy, covering comparative environmental legislation on issues such as biodiversity, food, nature rights, energy, wildlife trade, coasts, climate and water. Across such issues, the course will take an applied law and policy approach, including a focus on implementation and environmental enforcement domestically and globally.
Main topics
Global sustainability frameworks
Comparative environmental legislation on issues such as biodiversity, food, energy, wildlife trade, coasts, climate and water.
Designing, implementing and enforcing law and policy for sustainable development
