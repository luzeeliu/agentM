This course provides students with an in-depth understanding of the thermophysical properties, selection criteria, and performance characteristics of various coolant materials used in nuclear reactors. Students will investigate the heat transfer and hydraulic mechanisms that transmit power from the fuel to the turbine, enabling the safe and efficient operation of a power plant. Two-phase fluid flow and nucleate boiling will be discussed, with students examining strategies for managing coolant system integrity and reliability under normal and accident conditions.
