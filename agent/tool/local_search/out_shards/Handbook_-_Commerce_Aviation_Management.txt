The
Bachelor of Commerce and Bachelor of Aviation (Management)
are complementary programs, with Aviation providing a specific industry context in which to position broader skills developed within the Commerce degree. Graduates of the dual degree will be ideal candidates for the highly competitive graduate intake programs of Qantas and Virgin Australia airlines.

Final Year Synthesis (FYS)
keyboard_arrow_down
Students must complete their Final Year Synthesis (FYS) course within their final year of their Bachelor of Commerce program. That is, within 48 UOC or less remaining in their BCom program.
Students may meet more than one program requirement depending on their FYS course choice. For example; students who complete a FYS course that is also a Work Integrated Learning course within a major, will meet a major elective, WIL and FYS requirement.
Students who participate and complete significant extra-curricular projects (eg. Peter Farrell Cup), can apply for 0 UOC FYS (COMM3090).

Maximum Level 1 UOC
keyboard_arrow_down
A maximum of 72 UOC of Level 1 courses can be taken, including any General Education or mainstream Level 1 course taken to fulfil either the General Education or the Free Elective requirement.
any level 1 course
3835 - Course Substitution
keyboard_arrow_down
Students in this program should not take MATH1041. Students must take COMM1110 for their Commerce program to be counted towards satisfying the requirements to take MATH1041 in the Aviation program;
If MATH1031 or equivalent is completed under Aviation (Management) and the Commerce major requires ECON1202, then count MATH1031 under Aviation (Management) and complete another Commerce major elective to meet the 48uoc major requirement.

Limit Rule
keyboard_arrow_down
Students may complete up to a maximum of 60 UOC of any level 1 courses for the Commerce component of the double degree. FINS1612, MGMT1002 and COMM1822 completed within a major/minor may count as level 2 courses for the purpose of not exceeding the level 1 limit within the BCom program.
