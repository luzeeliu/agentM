Overview
This specialisation is designed to give students the opportunity to develop their area of expertise in Media, Intellectual Property and Technology Law by completing four courses (24 UOC) from the prescribed elective courses in Media, Intellectual Property and Technology Law specialisation course list. This specialisation is designed to give students the opportunity to develop their area of expertise in Media, Intellectual Property and Technology Law by completing four courses (24 UOC) from the prescribed elective courses in Media, Intellectual Property and Technology Law specialisation course list.
When a student completes the Master of Laws which incorporates a specialisation, the student's academic transcript and Testamur will identify the specialisation and will contain the words 'Master of Laws in [the specialisation completed]' or words to that effect.

Learning Outcomes
Expand all
1.
Analyse the legal frameworks, principles, and theories underpinning Technology, Intellectual Property and Media Law and governance.
keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Examine and critique the current and future role of law in contemporary global and national contexts, particularly in relation to technology, intellectual property, and media regulation.
keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Conduct advanced legal research that effectively uses a variety of primary and secondary sources and professional judgement to address challenges particularly in relation to emerging technologies, digital platforms, media regulation, and intellectual property issues.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Communicate knowledge of Technology, Intellectual Property and Media Law in both written and oral forms to a range of audiences, professionally and with precision.
