This program is for students commencing in 2022 onwards.
If you commenced prior to 2022, please refer to the program rules for the year you started.
The Master of Translation (MTran) is endorsed by the National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI). It is offered in two plans, 72 UoC (1.7 years, plan B) and 48 UoC (1 year, plan A). The program prepares you to sit for the Certified Translator certification exam administered by NAATI. With certain coursework, you can also sit the specialist certification exam for Certified Advanced Translator. The program will equip you with the necessary theoretical knowledge, practical skills, and technological capabilities to work as a competent and ethical translator in international and domestic settings. The areas of translation specialisation are multimedia, business, legal, medical, scientific, technical, subtitling and captioning, translation technology (machine translation, post-editing) and community translation. The program is staffed by leading researchers and practitioners in translation and linguistics. In our program, academic content is complemented by professional practical experience where you are able to apply what was learned in class in real professional settings. In addition to the knowledge and skills of translation as a discipline and as a profession, you will also learn related academic and practical skills such as critical analysis, independent research and preparation, project management and team work - which are all related to the work of translators. The program is offered in the following language combinations, subject to student demand: English and Chinese (Mandarin), Chinese (Cantonese), French, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, Russian, Serbian, Spanish and Turkish.
Fully online mode is only available to domestic students and students not on an international student visa.

Minimum Entry Requirements
keyboard_arrow_down
1 year (48 UOC) program (Specialisation A)
A bachelor's degree (or equivalent qualification) in any field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65) and an advanced level of bilingual proficiency*, plus one year relevant professional experience as a professional translator.
Students in this specialisation will receive 24 UOC in advanced standing upon entry to the program, and will complete a 1 year (48 UOC) program of study.
1.7 year (72 UOC) program (Specialisation B)
A bachelor's degree (or equivalent qualification) in any field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65) and an advanced level of bilingual proficiency*.
* You will be considered to have an advanced level of bilingual proficiency if you have:
Completed 3-year+ qualification equivalent to an Australian Bachelor degree which was undertaken in the nominated language, completed within the 10 years prior to commencement of the program applied for OR
Provided evidence of a NAATI certification/accreditation for interpreting or translation for the nominated language offered in this program OR
Successfully completed TAFE Diploma or TAFE Advanced Diploma of Interpreting OR
Successfully completed UNSW's I&T courses (MODL5100, MODL5101, MODL5102, MODL5103, MODL5104, MODL5107, MODL5108, MODL5112) OR
Provided evidence of being a native speaker of the language and completed high school in the nominated language for this program. High school transcript and proof of completion are required.
Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
Students who have completed the short courses below are eligible to receive up to 6 UoC per course towards their program subject to the requirements and limitations specified by the UNSW Recognition of Prior Learning Procedure.

SC0003 Advanced Legal Interpreting -> MODL5101 Interpreting in Legal Settings
SC0005 Introduction to Translation Technology -> MODL5107 Translation Technology
Students who have completed the following courses are also eligible to receive up to 6 UoC per course towards their program subject to the requirements and limitations specified by the UNSW Recognition of Prior Learning Procedure: MODL5100, MODL5103, MODL5104, MODL5107, MODL5108, MODL5112.
Limitations on Recognition of Prior Learning
keyboard_arrow_down
If you are seeking credit transfer, you must submit documentary evidence of courses completed at the time of initial enrolment. UNSW Arts, Design and Architecture will then determine the number of units of credit to be granted. Credit transfer will not be granted for courses completed more than ten years previously. The maximum credit transfer for a Masters is 50% of the specialisation, that is, up to 24 UOC for the 1 year specialisation and 36 UOC for the 1.7 year specialisation.

Professional Recognition
The program is endorsed by the National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) and also prepares students to sit the certification exams administered by NAATI for qualification as a Certified Provisional Interpreter, Certified Interpreter, and Certified Translator. With certain coursework, our program will also prepare students to sit specialist certification exams for Certified Advanced Translator, Certified Specialist Interpreter (Health), Certified Specialist Interpreter (Legal) and Certified Conference Interpreter.
Career Opportunities
The translation profession offers varied opportunities for employment both commercial and freelance, including translating, editing, subtitling, captioning, and technical writing. Translation services are valued in a number of government and commercial areas including legal, medical, business, and media.
