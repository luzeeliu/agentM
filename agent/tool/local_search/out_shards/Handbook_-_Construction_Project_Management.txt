Construction project management embraces the principles of project management and applies them across different phases of the construction project development cycle to achieve successful project outcomes in terms of time, cost, quality, safety, and sustainability. The program provides excellent opportunities for advanced study in construction project management in either full time or part time modes. The program aims at improving proficiency of practitioners in the construction industry to meet current and future challenges. You will learn about the general management and project management principles and technique, and how to apply them in managing construction projects.

Minimum Entry Requirements
keyboard_arrow_down
1.7 year (72 UOC) program
One of the following:
A qualification equivalent to an Australian Bachelor's degree in a related field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65)
A qualification equivalent to an Australian Bachelor's degree in any field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65), plus 2 years construction industry experience with some managerial responsibilities
A Graduate Certificate in Construction (or construction related fields) from an Australian institution with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65)
1 year (48 UOC) program
One of the following:
A qualification equivalent to an Australian undergraduate honours degree in a related field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65)
A qualification equivalent to an Australian undergraduate honours degree in any field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65), plus 2 years construction industry experience with some managerial responsibilities
A qualification equivalent to an Australian master's degree in a related field with a credit average (equivalent to a UNSW WAM of 65)
Students who meet one of the above criteria will receive 24 UOC in advanced standing upon entry to the program, and will complete a 1 year (48 UOC) program of study. They will not be required to undertake BENV7020 Research Seminar (6 UOC), REST0018 Research Project (12 UOC) and one 6 UOC elective.
Related fields:
Studies in built environment disciplines, engineering, business or law.
Definition of an honours degree:
An honours degree consists of usually a year-long research program as part of a bachelor's degree, either as an addition to a general bachelor’s degree or an embedded program in the final year of a specialised bachelor's degree. In the Honours degree, students shall complete selected courses within a supervised program of research (either field, laboratory, archival exploration or design based), and produce a high-quality research thesis or design work which is marked/graded. The Honours degree requires students to demonstrate advanced disciplinary knowledge through independent or self-directed research and critical thinking as required by the Australian Quality Framework (AQF) level 8.
Accepted roles at management level:
Architecture (Senior Level), Bidding Manager, Construction Manager, Contracts Administrator, Cost Manager/Cost Consultant, OH&S Manager, Project Engineer, Project Manager, Property Manager, Quantity Surveyor, Site Manager, Project Planner/Scheduler (Senior Level), Project Risk Manager, Project Management Office (PMO) Manager

Career Opportunities
The program is appropriate for people seeking careers in construction management, project management, design management, value management and international project management. It also provides valuable education to those seeking a broader base to careers in architecture, engineering, property development, urban planning and facilities management.

The Award with Excellence is awarded in coursework masters programs, including Masters (Extension) but with the exception of Masters (Extended) such as JD and MD, when a Weighted Average Mean (WAM) of at least 80% has been achieved and at least 50% of the requirements of the award are completed at UNSW. All eligible programs will award 'with Excellence' except in special circumstances where approval of Academic Board has been given for a program to opt out.
