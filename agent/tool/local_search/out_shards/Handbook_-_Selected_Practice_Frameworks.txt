This course introduces you to selected key therapy frameworks (for example- family therapy, narrative, cognitive, behavioural, interpersonal-psychotherapy, and /or mindfulness- based frameworks) that may be used to inform contemporary social work counselling practice. You will develop a critical understanding of the theoretical and skills base of these frameworks and apply the ideas and practices to contexts of social work counselling. The interactive style of teaching in this course and the course assessment program will provide opportunities for you to develop more advanced capacities in reflective practice by identifying and developing the potential applications of ideas and practices from these frameworks to your own counselling practice.
