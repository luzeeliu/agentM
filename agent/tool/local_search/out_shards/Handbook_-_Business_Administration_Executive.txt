The AGSM MBA (Executive) degree is designed for experienced professionals. The degree will equip you with the knowledge, skills and connections that you need to accelerate your career progression and take on new challenges in your current industry or in a new industry or sector. The core of the program helps ensure that you have the necessary contemporary business knowledge and skills required in our rapidly changing environment. In addition, you can choose from a wide range of elective courses to ensure that the program is tailored to your specific needs and interests. This includes the opportunity to participate in AGSM's international offerings.
Courses are delivered in a range of modes to ensure that you can fit study around your personal, work and other commitments. In these courses you will be working with skilled facilitators and studying with experienced colleagues from diverse backgrounds.
The most distinctive feature of the AGSM MBA (Executive) Program is the Executive Year, with residential courses delivered over three consecutive terms. You will participate with the same cohort in a transformational learning experience that will help you develop the leadership skills and strategic insights that contemporary executives need in an accelerating world.
You will be able to apply the concepts and tools encountered in the program to your professional life immediately, while preparing for great things in the future – accelerating your current career, following a new path or building a new enterprise.
The program is designed to be undertaken part-time in combination with full-time employment.

Minimum Entry Requirements
keyboard_arrow_down
To be eligible to be admitted to the MBA (Executive) Program, students must meet the following minimum entry criteria:
A Bachelor’s degree (or equivalent) in any discipline plus at least 5 years' relevant professional experience
OR
For students without a previous degree, at least 5 years' relevant professional experience plus a minimum of 5 years' managerial experience with accountability for outcomes, projects and people. Your CV will need to effectively demonstrate this.
Limitations on Recognition of Prior Learning
keyboard_arrow_down
Previous study can lead to Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL). When a student demonstrates their knowledge in a specific course, AGSM Programs may award credit for that subject. RPL is not available for the Executive Year (EY) courses. Please see the UNSW Recognition of Prior Learning (Coursework Programs) Procedure for further details.
