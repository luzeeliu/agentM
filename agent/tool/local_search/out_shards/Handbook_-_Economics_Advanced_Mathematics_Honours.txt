Overview
The Bachelor of Economics /Bachelor of Science (Advanced Mathematics) (Honours) program enables students to complete an in-depth course of study in advanced mathematics from within UNSW Science which complements the economics program offered by the School of Economics in the UNSW Business School.
The
Bachelor of Economics /Bachelor of Science (Advanced Mathematics) (Honours)
program enables students to complete an in-depth course of study in advanced mathematics from within UNSW Science which complements the economics program offered by the School of Economics in the UNSW Business School.

Double Degree Structure
Students must complete 240 UOC.
These UOC are made up of:
Economics Component (96 UOC)
Introductory Business Core (6 UOC)
Economics Core Courses (48 UOC)
Economics Electives (30 UOC)
Business School Electives (12 UOC)
Students may choose to use their Economics core and elective courses to complete an optional Economics major. Courses for the major are taken once but also meet core and elective course rules where listed.
Bachelor of Advanced Mathematics (Honours) (144 UOC)
1. An approved Advanced Mathematics (Honours) major and
2. SCIF1131;
3. 48 units of credit Honours Year; and
4. Science elective courses

Special arrangements for Mathematics and Economics Courses
keyboard_arrow_down
Students should do the MATH courses which are required in the Advanced Maths (Honours) specialisations , ie MATH1141 & MATH1241. These courses will substitute for Economics courses in your major as follows:-
MATH1141 will substitute for ECON1202.
MATH1241 will substitute for ECON1203.
Students must count the MATH courses towards the Advanced Maths (Honours) component of the degree.Additional Economics courses will be required to ensure a minimum of 78 UOC of Economics courses are completed.

Optional Economics Major
48 Units of Credit:
keyboard_arrow_down
Students may choose to complete one or two optional Economics majors.  Students may use their Economics Core and Economics Elective courses to complete these economics majors. Please note: If you choose to study two optional Economics majors, you must choose another 6 courses (36 UOC) from the second major that do not overlap with the first optional major.
