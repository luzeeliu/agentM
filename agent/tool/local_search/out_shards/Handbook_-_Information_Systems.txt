The Information Systems (IS) minor is tailored to students who are interested in business and management aspects of the discipline. You will learn about business IS in various complex decision making contexts and how these systems, and the information generated by them, support the modern organisation in achieving both day-to-day and long-term objectives. Graduates from this specialisation have a range of broad career paths to choose including: Business Analyst Consulting; Forensics and intelligence; New Product/Service Development; Internet Entrepreneurship; Systems Development; Communications and networking; Information Systems Security; Project Management; and Information Technology Management.

Specialisation Structure
Students must complete 30 UOC.
Students taking a combination of majors and/or minors that require both COMM2822 or INFS1603 and COMP3311 as core courses, should take only COMP3311, and replace COMM2822/INFS1603 with an additional major or minor elective.
Students taking a combination of major and/or minors in Business Analytics and Information Systems can complete either INFS1603 or COMM2822 as the core course and fulfill the major UOC requirement by completing an additional prescribed elective for the major or minor.
