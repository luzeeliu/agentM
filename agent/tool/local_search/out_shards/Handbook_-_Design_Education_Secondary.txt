This program is for students commencing from 2026.
The double degree program, Bachelor of Design/Bachelor of Education (Secondary) is a 4 year program (192 UOC) for intending secondary school teachers. The program is a nationally accredited pre-service qualification.
The program covers both theoretical and practical aspects of education, including 80 days supervised professional experience in at least two different secondary schools.
The
Computational Design
specialisation enables students to gain teaching accreditation in:
Information Processes and Technology (first or second teaching area)
Design and Technology (first or second teaching area)
The
Industrial Design
specialisation enables students to gain teaching accreditation in:
Design and Technology (first teaching area only)
Graphics and Multimedia Technology (second teaching area only)
The
Integrated Design
specialisation enables students to gain teaching accreditation in:
Visual Arts (first teaching area only)
Graphics and Multimedia Technology (optional second teaching area)
Textiles and Fashion (optional second teaching area)

Specialisation - Computational Design
keyboard_arrow_down
A specialisation in Computational Design enables you to pursue teaching areas in Information Processes and Technology, and Design and Technology. You must complete the following teaching method courses: Computing Technology Method 1 and 2; and Design and Technology Method 1 and 2.
Specialisation - Industrial Design
keyboard_arrow_down
A specialisation in Industrial Design enables you to pursue teaching areas in Design and Technology, and Graphics and Multimedia Technology. You must complete the following teaching method courses: Design and Technology Method 1 and 2; and Graphics and Multimedia Method 1 and 2.
Specialisation - Integrated Design
keyboard_arrow_down
A specialisation in Integrated Design enables you to pursue a teaching area in Visual Arts, with the option of pursuing a second teaching area in either Graphics and Multimedia Technology or Textiles and Fashion Design.
To meet NESA accreditation requirements for Visual Arts, you must choose one 2D (Graphics) AND one 3D/4D disciplinary studio stream (Object (3D), 3D Visualisation, Experience, Textiles, Fashion, or Interaction (4D)), for your Integrated Design Disciplinary Studios.
If you choose to pursue only Visual Arts as your teaching area, you must complete the following method courses: Visual Arts Method 1 and 2; Extension Visual Arts Method 1 and 2.
If you choose to pursue Visual Arts and Graphics and Multimedia Technology as your teaching areas, you must complete the following method courses: Visual Arts Method 1 and 2; Graphics and Multimedia Method 1 and 2.
If you choose to pursue Visual Arts and Textiles and Fashion Design as your teaching areas, you must take Textiles as your 3D/4D disciplinary studio, and you must complete the following method courses: Visual Arts Method 1 and 2; Textiles and Design Method 1 and 2.
