Industrial Design Theory A introduces you to the principles of designing for people. The course focuses on access, usability, comfort, and performance of the human-product interaction system. You will learn strategies for representing and engaging users in the design process, drawing on a variety of perspectives including human factors, anthropometry, biomechanics, cognitive ergonomics, user experience (UX), user interface (UI), universal design, and inclusive design. In addition, you will investigate the human-use implications of your current and future design practice. The course aims to help you become better designers through understanding the needs and diversity of the users of products, places, and systems shaped through design practice.
