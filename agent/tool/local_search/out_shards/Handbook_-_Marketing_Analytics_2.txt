Today’s data-rich environment and the advance of analytics tools have changed the direction of marketing and consumer behaviour in almost every industry. Today’s marketing manager must use data analytics to better understand the consumer purchase and consumption journey. Analytics helps improve marketing effectiveness by offering the right products, prices, and other marketing efforts (e.g., personalised advertising) at the right times, and to the right customers.
Marketing, product, brand, sales, advertising, and PR managers as well as entrepreneurs must incorporate analytics tools in their marketing decision-making process.  However, industry has difficulty in finding the right marketing analytics talent who understands both marketing and data analytics.
This specialisation in Marketing Analytics provides students with actionable marketing analytics training for marketing decision-making by offering students a range of analytical tools to address daily marketing problems and emerging marketing needs in a world of big data.

Recommended Program Electives
keyboard_arrow_down
Students in the Marketing Analytics specialisation are strongly recommended to complete COMM5007 as their MCom program elective to complement their specialisation; particularly prior to completing MARK5826 and MARK5828.
Course Sharing for Double Specialisations
keyboard_arrow_down
Students who complete two specialisations that share a common Foundation Core Course (COMMKS and ECONFS, FINSCS and FINSQS, or MARKGS and MARKTS) must take an additional 6 UOC of Electives or Capstone Courses. These students are strongly recommended to choose another specialisation course as their additional Elective.
For example, in Marketing, and Marketing Analytics where MARK5700 are requirements for both specialisations, students are recommended to complete an additional specialisation core elective course – e.g. MARK5822/MARK5826/MARK5827/MARK5824/ MARK5825/MARK5810, etc
