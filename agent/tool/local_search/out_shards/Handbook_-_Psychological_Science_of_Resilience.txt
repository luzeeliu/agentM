This course offers an introduction to the scientific research, theory and application concerning “resilience” as a key factor in life and learning success and wellbeing. It will provide an introduction to key concepts and skills underpinning student success, including positive psychology, self-knowledge, academic competence, psychological flexibility, and connectedness and communication. This course has flipped classroom (FC) delivery, where instead of attending two lectures, you are expected to undertake a minimum of 2 hrs work prior to each practical, where experienced lecturers will engage interactively with you and the material.
