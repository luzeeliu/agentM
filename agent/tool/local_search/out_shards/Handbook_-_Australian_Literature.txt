This course introduces you to major issues and works in Australian literature, with a particular focus on material from the past two centuries. It will contextualise important works in terms of a range of local and global literary and cultural formations, thus providing a map of the complex and changing nature of national culture. You will study material by writers from diverse backgrounds, including Indigenous writers. You will also engage with the larger public culture of Australian Literature by considering the place of literature in contemporary life. If you are completing  a program in Education Studies, you will be offered the option of pursuing your study of Australian Literature through a pedagogical rubric.
