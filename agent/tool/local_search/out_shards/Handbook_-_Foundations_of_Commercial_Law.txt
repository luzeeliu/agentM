This course provides an introduction to foundational principles of commercial relationships in a Common Law system. It examines key concepts that shape these private law relationships, such as:
Agency
Consumer Law
Torts of relevance to commercial relationships
Fiduciary duties, unconscionability and other equitable concepts
Principles of property in a commercial context
Applied contract law in a commercial context.
The focus of this course is to provide a comprehensive grounding to LLM students who have not undertaken legal study in a Common Law jurisdiction.
Please note that this course is not intended to satisfy the requirements for admission to practice in Australia and cannot be used for credit in the LLB or Juris Doctor.
This course is not intended for students holding a Bachelor of Law or Juris Doctor qualification from an Australian or Common Law jurisdiction.
