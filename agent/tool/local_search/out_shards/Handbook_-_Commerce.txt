The
Bachelor of Commerce (BCom)
is a highly valued business qualification offering students a career-focused education for long term success. Students are empowered to become adaptive thinkers and drive purposeful change to thrive in a fast-changing world.
A unique feature of the BCom is the Integrated First Year, where students learn about business management and the broader environment in which an organisation operates, with a strong focus on developing professional skills and capabilities.
In this flexible, diverse and comprehensive business degree, students have exclusive access to
Career Accelerator
– an impressive suite of career-boosting opportunities. Completion of at least one for-credit Career Accelerator course is a signature feature of the BCom.
Students complete up to two majors, selecting from a comprehensive and industry advised suite, including Accounting, Business Analytics, Economics, Finance, FinTech, HR, Information Systems, International Business, Marketing and Taxation, many fulfilling professional accreditation requirements.
The
myBCom Series
, designed in collaboration with industry and alumni, guides students to develop a personalised roadmap to get the full benefit of the curricular and co-curricular opportunities offered in the BCom. Students graduate with their personal digital myBCom Graduate Portfolio, showcasing their academic, technical and practical career-ready skills and capabilities for a fulfilling career.
High performing students can study an additional Honours year or more advanced postgraduate studies.

Program Structure
Students must complete 144 UOC when taken as a standalone program.
These UOC are made up of:
Integrated First Year Courses (48 UOC)
myBCom (0 UOC)
Commerce major (48 UOC)
Optional major/minor or free electives (36 UOC)
General Education (12 UOC)
Degree Requirements
Students must also meet the following requirements by completing:
Work Integrated Learning (WIL) – 6 UOC*
Final Year Synthesis (FYS) – 6 UOC *
Within the above program structure, students must ensure that they complete a minimum of 96 Units of Credit (UOC) of courses offered by the UNSW Business School. Some courses overlap between the Integrated First Year and Commerce majors which allows students in the Bachelor of Commerce to complete a double major.
*Some courses overlap between Commerce major, Work Integrated Learning, and Final Year Synthesis requirements. Where there is an overlap, a single course may fulfil multiple of these requirements and students must complete an additional Business School elective (any course offered by the UNSW Business School) to ensure that they will meet the minimum 96 UOC of Business courses. Where an overlap occurs and a student will already have met the 96 UOC Business course requirement, students must take any Free Elective course instead.
Please see the 'Double Majors' drop-down section below for more information.

Double Majors
keyboard_arrow_down
If a student chooses to complete 2 commerce majors and the majors have a Integrated First Year (IFY) core course in common, they are permitted to double count a maximum of 6 UOC of this overlap unless specified otherwise.
Students completing a major sequence with courses in common which are not part of the IFY courses may only count the overlap towards one major and must replace this course within the other major with a major elective of the same level or higher (e.g. Level 3 core course for a Level 3 major elective).

Final Year Synthesis (FYS)
keyboard_arrow_down
Students must complete their Final Year Synthesis (FYS) course within their final year of their Bachelor of Commerce program. That is, within 48 UOC or less remaining in their BCom program.
Students may meet more than one program requirement depending on their FYS course choice. For example; students who complete a FYS course that is also a Work Integrated Learning course within a major, will meet a major elective, WIL and FYS requirement.
Students who participate and complete significant extra-curricular projects (eg. Peter Farrell Cup), can apply for 0 UOC FYS (COMM3090).

Accreditations
Expand all
Association of Chartered Certified Accountants
keyboard_arrow_down
The Bachelor of Commerce at UNSW is accredited by the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA).
Graduates who complete this program may eligible to apply for exemptions from the Applied Knowledge and Applied Skills exams of the ACCA qualification and proceed with the Strategic Professional exams. With full exemption, completion of the Strategic Professional exams and the Ethics and Professional Skills module, graduates will attain Affiliate status with ACCA. 36 months of relevant working experience will be required to become a full ACCA member. To find out your eligibility for exemption, please see the
ACCA webpage
.
Australian Human Resource Institute
keyboard_arrow_down
Current students and graduates of the Human Resource Management major are eligible to apply for professional membership with the Australian HR Institute (AHRI). For further information regarding AHRI, please see
https://www.ahri.com.au/
.
CPA Australia
keyboard_arrow_down
Associate membership of CPA Australia may be obtained with a Bachelor of Commerce or similar degree that incorporates a major in Accounting, and completion of the other accredited courses to satisfy its core knowledge requirements. A list of the required courses can be accessed through
https://www.cpaaustralia.com.au/ManageApplications/AccreditedCourse.mvc/SearchAccreditedCourses
Chartered Accountants Australia and New Zealand
keyboard_arrow_down
Graduates who have completed the required accredited courses in a UNSW undergraduate degree with a major in Accounting are eligible to enter the Chartered Accountants Program leading to membership. Students may confirm academic entry requirements for the 'Chartered Accountants Program' with Chartered Accountants Australia and New Zealand at
www.charteredaccountantsanz.com/become-a-member/entry-requirements/chartered-accountant
Chartered Institute of Management Accountants
keyboard_arrow_down
The Chartered Institute of Management Accountants (CIMA) has a qualification framework comprising 16 exams and a practical experience requirement. BCom graduates majoring in Accounting receive 8 exemptions. After passing the remaining 8 exams and meeting the experience requirement graduates may become members of CIMA with the right to use the CGMA designation (Chartered Global Management Accountant). For further information on the specific exemptions see
http://www.cimaglobal.com/Starting-CIMA/Starting-CIMA/Exemptions/Exemption-Search/UNSW-Sydney-14804/?qualification=1-62W87QH
Professional Recognition
Graduates are eligible to apply for membership of various professional organisations according to the area of specialisation completed. For further information on professional recognition please contact UNSW Business School (https://www.unsw.edu.au/business/about-us/accreditation)
