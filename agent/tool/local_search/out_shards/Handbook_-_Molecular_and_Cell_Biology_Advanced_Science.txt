Recent advances in Molecular Cellular Biology, especially the continuing development of recombinant DNA technology, have revolutionised our understanding of the structure, function and regulation of individual genes. These advances have opened up the exciting field of Molecular Biology, one of the most rapid growth areas in biology. This marriage of Biochemistry, Microbiology, and Cell Biology provides an exciting new approach for the study of all living organisms, including the human. Molecular Biology therefore represents fundamental components of biological and medical science and they will have increasingly important roles to play in many aspects of modern medicine, genetics, evolutionary biology, bioinformatics, biotechnology and genomics. This Molecular and Cell Biology major offers a general introduction to the discipline during the first years of study and then allows students to diversify into the more specialised areas in later years.

Learning Outcomes
Expand all
1.
Demonstrate an in-depth understanding of the fundamental principles of molecular and cell biology, including the organization and function of cells from birth to death and the molecular mechanisms of the regulation of cellular processes.
keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Develop statistical, computational and laboratory skills required for investigation into the complexity of molecular and cellular processes.
keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Critically review and analyse scientific literature, derive hypotheses, design and perform experimental approaches, and analyse and communicate the findings through publications, presentations and digital media.
keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Analyse expression of genes and regulation of protein and lipid synthesis, trafficking, degradation and function in cells in health and disease.
keyboard_arrow_down
5.
Collaborate effectively with different groups to incorporate diverse perspectives and experiences for the purpose of investigating and solving complex problems in molecular cell biology.
keyboard_arrow_down
6.
Develop a wider perspective on molecular and cell biology to understand the fundamental role they play in other disciplines such as genetics and biotechnology and develop awareness of ethical and social issues in addressing global health challenges.
